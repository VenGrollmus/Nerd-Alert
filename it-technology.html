Blockchain and Cryptocurrencies

By Patrick

What does it do?

The idea of blockchain technology is to record information and to make it almost impossible to change, hack or manipulate. A blockchain is a list of records linked together using cryptography (Greek meaning – Kryptos “hidden, secret” and graphein “to write”) which contains information from the previous block or message. There are three components of each message. The first component is the cryptographic hash containing details of the previous block, which is an algorithm that maps arbitrary size data to a fixed size called the “hash value”. The hash value then references a has table which references keys to values, these are one-way functions that are practically impossible to invert or reverse. The second component is a timestamp, which keeps track of the creation and modification time of the document. This is a layer of security where no one can change the message not even the owner once the message has been created. The third component is the transaction data which is generally represented as a Merkle tree. A Merkle tree encodes the blockchain in an efficient and secure manner.

A blockchain is therefore a digital ledger of transactions consisting of blocks that are used to record transactions across multiple computers to ensure that these transactions are secure and cannot be altered, without altering all the subsequent blocks. Blockchain databases are managed on a peer-to-peer networks. These networks are computers that are equally permissioned and have the same level of responsibility to process data. Blockchain technology can be described in the forms of an inverted pyramid, the more layers are added the more secure the encrypted messages become as each layer added includes details of the previous layer.

In the next 3 years or less there is talk about a blockchain revolution, which sounds rather dramatic, however eight out of the world ten largest companies are building an array of products that incorporate blockchain technology. Companies recording and overseeing transactions stand to benefit from using blockchain technology. Blockchain technology has already been adopted by a number if industries like, finance, retail, real-estate, logistics and health care but to name a few.

Although the birth of blockchain technology was mainly surrounding cryptocurrencies it has now moved to other industries due to its secure cost-effective nature and opportunities presented. It is on the verge of replacing many other technologies as the preferred option. The bigger the blockchain the longer the time taken to create the next block within the chain and the more secure the block and the more complicated it would be hack the chain.

The move to blockchain technology is on the rise as only 1% of CIOs in 2018 adopted the technology and this has increase to 5% in 2019, believe this is the game changer for the future.

What is the likely impact?

Blockchain technology has the ability to revolutionise the way we do business and replace the current technologies we have and use. There are a range of benefits on offer and something to consider which include the enhancement of security, greater transparency, reduction of costs, increased efficiencies, speed of transactions and improved traceability. The technology is currently strongly affecting the financial services industry especially the accounting, auditing and banking sector allowing cross-border transactions in a shot amount of time without having to incur currency exchange fees.

Blockchain technology has the potential to change a number of different industries. At this this stage it is currently used in the financial sector to trade digital currencies like Bitcoin, however it also has to potential to be used in things like, the clearing, settlement and reporting of trades in the financial markets. Nearly 40 institutions have formed a Post Trade Ledger Working Group to determine to feasibility of using this technology to facilitate these processes. This technology has also been considered in mortgage industry and cross boarder trading. There are a number of other industries that are exploring the feasibility of block chain technology, like, Shipping and Supply Chain, Proxy Voting and Elections, Healthcare and even the government but to name a few.

With blockchain technology there will be a level of increased automation, therefore a decrease in the number of jobs that required manual verification of documents and processes. As this relates to more efficient processes, there will be a reduction in these types of jobs and services to machines, however there will be a requirement for new skills to be learnt to fill the gaps created by these changes. It is important to recognise that technological changes of the past have made many jobs unnecessary but have in turn created even more jobs in different sectors, just creating different opportunities rather that economic uncertainty.

How will this affect you?

The world is ever changing and so is technology and the need to keep up is key to managing your day-to-day living. I think blockchain technology is here to stay and it is critical to understand how this will impact you and your loved ones. At this stage I believe there is a small component of people who truly understand how these technologies work and how they impact our daily lives. With the changes implemented by blockchain technology, I believe that the way we get paid will soon be in digital currency not only based on its flexibility but also because of its convenience and security. Currently there is a level of decentralisation and the non-commitment on the side of the central banks to adopt the currency based on compliance and regulations concerns, however I believe that this will change in the future.

As the idea behind blockchain technology is a secure medium of transacting peer-to-peer, this not only relates to money but also assets. It is my view that the older generation like my parents will be left behind as they will not understand the new technology and the processes. In terms and me this will require a better understanding of what is possible and how. In terms of my children this will just be the way of the world and will become part of their normal day to day living.

In some ways this will become the new norm in others it is concerning, as the world forgets the simplicity of life and the real reasons for living. My biggest concerns for myself and my family are that the more we change the easier things become and the less resilient we become.

Blockchain is here to stay, like it or not. The say the more things change the more they stay the same, or do they?

Natural Language Processing and Natural Language Understanding
This development in Natural Language processing can be used to allow the chatbot to recognize user intent efficiently, decode the mood of users instantly, and drive the flow of conversations in tune with customer’s emotions. The study of NLP is broad as there are many algorithms to support different operations to process language such as extraction, tokenize, stop words, and response matching. Extraction selects the important keys words of the input received and summarises the query further according to those key words to understand the query. Tokenize will separate each word or sentence as “tokens” to allow easy matching (5 Simple Ways to Tokenize Text in Python, 2022). Stop words are considered low level information, in this process they are identified and removed from the input as they are normally interchangeable and not relevant, words such as “the”, “a”, “so”, “what” (Text pre-processing: Stop words removal using different libraries, 2022). Stemming will remove a portion of the word down to its root meaning (Stemming? Lemmatization? What? 2022). Response matching in a rule-based system, you can have a set of Reponses and the response matching process will process the input and match the appropriate response. It is also possible to leverage the chatbot sentiment analysis feature to easily know if customers are having a pleasant experience with your chatbots (End to End Question-Answering System Using NLP and SQuAD Dataset, 2022).

Cloud Services

By Ven

What does it do?

What is the state of the art of this new technology?

Cloud Computing and Services is the newish paradigm, even though it has been around for approximately 15 years now, for the hosting of delivering of services over the internet. Some of the services that are available is data storage, application hosting such as Microsoft Office, and video/movie sharing services such as Plex, Netflix, and Prime Video.

What can be done now?

Cloud computing is attractive to business owners as it eliminates the requirement for users to plan ahead for provisioning and allows enterprises to start from the small and increase resources only when there is a rise in service demand.

Cloud Computing has evolved from infrastructure, a place to store data, to platforms that run applications to now being able to develop cloud-native services. The major suppliers in the cloud services realm are Amazon, Microsoft and Google. They have always understood the importance of data for businesses and wanted to make it affordable for businesses to migrate and store their data and have made it affordable. The players also understood that systems and processing would follow.

This is something businesses and individuals need to keep in mind when planning their core cloud destination for the data. Even if the advanced services aren’t used initially, this could be a long-term relationship which will need to included in your decision making when looking at the core data location.

Other services that are available is the ability to teleconference, by using applications such as Zoom and Microsoft Teams. With this software we have the ability for video conferencing, being able to record the meetings and sharing screens to manage projects and discuss issues with software that may not be performing or operating as expected.

What is likely to be able to do be done soon (say in the next 3 years)?

Over the next three (3) years, I see ‘cloud-native’ applications/services being developed, when incorporated together, will deliver solutions for businesses with flexibility and scalability for them to be able to push the boundaries of innovation in the field they compete in.

I believe that a crucial part of this will be virtualisation of operating systems rather than the traditional virtual machines that mimicked entire computers or servers. It makes the overall system more efficient at application hosting, and the flexibility and easier to move them to wherever you want them to be; if that is moving from one provider to another, or from one data centre to another.

What is important to understand is how to deploy new ideas and innovations to the cloud, and leveraging the power of the platform, and being able to do it quickly and safely without the possibility of opening security issues from poorly written code that will allow nefarious users to access details or to place malware/viruses onto your cloud environment which would cause issues to the business either at the data or financial level.

The technological and developmental improvements that have made these services possible is the improvement in internet access, esp. with the advent of the NBN, 5G, and Wi-Fi 6; as this relates to the speed of accessing data. IT Hardware has also seen an improvement in the manufacturing of the equipment, processing speed of the hardware and storage availability either being SATA drives or Solid-State Drives.
